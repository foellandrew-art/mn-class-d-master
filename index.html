⚠️ The Risk Profile

Novice drivers face significantly higher crash risks during their first year of licensure. The lack of experience in hazard anticipation, combined with distractions (especially passengers and phones), leads to critical errors in judgment.

🛡️ The Solution: GDL & Supervision

Minnesota's Graduated Driver Licensing (GDL) system addresses these specific risks by legally mandating 50 hours of supervised practice (15 at night) and restricting high-risk scenarios (late-night driving, peer passengers) during the Provisional phase.

"Even after your teen is licensed, use your time driving with them to instill habits of good judgment, accurate hazard anticipation, and smart decision-making."

⚖️ Vanessa's Law (Minn. Stat. § 171.05)

A strict penalty for unlicensed teen drivers. If an unlicensed teen receives a crash-related moving violation or an alcohol/controlled substance violation, they cannot be given a license, instruction permit, or provisional license until age 18. At 18, they must pass the knowledge test, fulfill reinstatement requirements, and hold a permit for six months.

⏱️ The Basic Speed Law

Statutory limits (10mph alleys, 30mph urban, 55mph rural) apply when signs are absent. However, the Basic Speed Law dictates you must never drive faster than is reasonable under existing conditions. 55mph in a blizzard is illegal.

🔀 The Zipper Merge

When a lane is closed ahead, stay in your current lane up to the point of merge. Then take turns with the other lane (1 to 1). Merging early causes unpredictable behavior and worsens traffic backups.

Scenario: Sign says "Right Lane Ends 1 Mile."
Action: Stay in the right lane. Drive to the merge point. Take turns. Do not force yourself into the left lane a mile early.

🛑 Uncontrolled Intersections

If two vehicles arrive at an intersection without signs/signals at the same time, the driver on the left must yield to the driver on the right.

DWI & Not a Drop Law

The legal limit for 21+ is 0.08. However, Minnesota enforces a "Not a Drop" policy for drivers under 21. Any detectable amount of alcohol results in license suspension. Implied Consent means by driving, you agree to breath/blood testing; refusal yields a 1-year revocation.

🚛 Commercial Vehicles & No-Zones

Large trucks have massive blind spots on all four sides. Rule of thumb: If you cannot see the driver's face in their side mirror, they cannot see you. They also require wide turning radiuses; never pass a turning truck on the right.

🌧️ Hydroplaning (Minn. Stat. § 169.13)

Water can collect under tires causing loss of contact with the road at speeds as low as 35 mph. Action: Take foot off the gas. Do NOT brake hard. Keep steering wheel straight.

🚌

School Bus Stop Law

When a school bus is stopped with its red lights flashing and stop arm extended, you MUST stop your vehicle at least 20 feet away from the bus. This applies to both following and opposing traffic, unless you are on the opposite side of a physically separated roadway (like a concrete median). Violations are heavily penalized.
